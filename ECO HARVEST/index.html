NASA's Role in Agriculture

NASA uses satellites to collect data on weather patterns, soil moisture, vegetation health, and climate conditions. This information helps farmers make better decisions about planting, irrigation, and resource management.

Key Technologies Used

  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index): Measures plant health and growth
  • Satellite Imagery: High-resolution images of Earth's surface
  • Weather Forecasting: Real-time climate data for planning
  • Soil Moisture Monitoring: Tracks water availability in soil

Real-World Applications

This game demonstrates how space technology can revolutionize farming, helping to increase food production while protecting the environment. NASA data helps prevent crop failures, optimize water use, and promote sustainable agriculture worldwide.
